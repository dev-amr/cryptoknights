What is Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is a new kind of money. It's the first electronic currency not controlled by a single organization or government. There are currently (sep ’14) over 2 Million user wallets in the Bitcoin network. All over the world people are exchanging hundreds of thousands of pounds worth of bitcoin every day with no middle man and no credit card companies. A user to user (Peer2Peer) network is made up of computers like yours so no bank or payment processor is required between you and whoever you're exchanging Bitcoins with, whether your buying something, sending money to friends or family or you’re a business who accepts Bitcoin. This decentralization (not being managed by a central computer and authority) is the basis for Bitcoin's Security and Freedom. Bitcoin is basically more efficient than regular money because it can flow more freely between users – across a room or from one side of the globe to the other. Also, Bitcoin has no £5/£10 minimum in shops or on websites, when all you want is a small coffee or a days’ worth of site access, unlike a regular credit or debit card. There can only ever be 21,000,000 Bitcoins and currently there have been around 12,500,000 mined into use. You can’t print more of it, you can’t devalue it like other fiat currencies and so the value of Bitcoin is derived from its ability to provide service to its users. It’s not backed by a government or bank, it’s backed by the people that use it as a better way to transport value around the planet between people and business.
